**Category**: semantics · **Expected kind**: analytical · **Confidence**: `inferred` · **Mode**: deterministic

## Q13 — What does the range-row treatment mean?

**Category**: semantics · **Expected kind**: analytical · **Confidence**: `inferred` · **Mode**: deterministic


### Answer

Range-form GL rows (e.g. `'3001-06'`, `'0009-12'`, `'0172-175'`) are summary postings that span multiple lots — typically shared-shell or shared-infrastructure costs that genuinely cannot be attributed to one physical lot. Memo evidence (`'shell allocation'`), per-row dollar magnitude (~$3-14K), and design / engineering vendor mix all confirm this interpretation. v2.1 keeps these rows at the project+phase grain via `vf_unattributed_shell_dollars` per phase, totaling 4,020 rows / $45,752,047 across 8 VF codes (HarmTo, LomHT1, PWFT1, ArroT1, MCreek, SaleTT, SaleTR, WilCrk). They are NOT allocated to specific lots in v2.1. Per-lot expansion is a v2.2 candidate that needs source-owner sign-off on the allocation method (equal split vs sales-price-weighted vs unit-fixed). When a query asks 'what is the total cost for lot X?', the v2.1 answer is the lot's `vf_actual_cost_3tuple_usd` plus an explicit reference to the phase-level `vf_unattributed_shell_dollars` ('plus a share of $X phase shell costs that haven't been allocated to specific lots yet').
